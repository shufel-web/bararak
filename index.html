Banana Wine – Basic Procedure

Equipment

  • Large pot
  • Strainer or cloth
  • Fermentation bottle (demijohn)
  • Airlock

Ingredients

  • 1.2 kg ripe bananas
  • 200 g sultanas
  • 900 g sugar
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 black tea bag
  • Wine yeast

Steps

  1. Boil fruit: Chop bananas (with skins) and add to a pot with sultanas and about 4 L of water. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  2. Mash: Mash the fruit in the hot water to release flavor.
  3. Add lemon & tea: Add lemon juice and a cup of brewed black tea. Stir well.
  4. Strain: Strain the liquid into a clean container, removing solids.
  5. Add sugar: Stir in sugar while the liquid is still hot until fully dissolved.
  6. Cool: Let the liquid cool to room temperature.
  7. Ferment: Pour into a fermentation bottle, add yeast, and fit an airlock.
  8. Wait: Leave to ferment in a warm, dark place for several weeks until bubbling stops.
  9. Bottle: Once fermentation is completely finished and the wine has cleared, bottle it.
